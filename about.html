Lewis Darnton

  • Designer
I want to work with people that share the same passion, determination, and vision as I do.

In my design approach, I am a firm believer that the most effective solutions arise from well-developed minimalistic ideas - deriving complexity from simplicity.

I embrace the opportunity to learn new skills and as such many of my personal ventures have arisen from a problem or need I have identified, pursued and developed. The wide range of projects on this website exhibit my ability to fully immerse myself in the task at hand and develop compelling solutions.

An education in Architectural Design has allowed me to develop key design skills in communication, critical thinking, and problem solving. In my own time, I have further developed my skill set, self-teaching basic coding skills and learning relevant software as required. This has given me a set of transferable skills that can be applied to a broad range of design disciplines.

November 2015 - Present

From the professional experience of working in a thriving architectural practice, I have developed further ability in time-management, teamwork and working under pressure.

During my time as an Architectural Assistant I have worked across multiple projects at various stages of the design cycle. This varies from inception, where I have overseen the design of a set of luxury houses that achieved planning permission by the local authority, to the detailed design of construction tender packages.

September 2012 - July 2015

Whilst studying at Newcastle University, I was challenged with developing numerous design briefs from the concept stage through to producing coherent design proposals.

The design process for each project included weekly one-on-one design tutorials and interim critiques that prefaced a final critique. This process involved presenting design work to peers and teachers from various design studios to gather opinions and advice.

The design projects included a variety of restrictions which help to inform the proposed design, here I gained skills in self-critique and design analysis.

September 2003 - July 2011
