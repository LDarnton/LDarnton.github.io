Architectural Design

HOUSING DESIGN

The National Institute for Medical Research occupied the site in Barnet, London, since the nineteen-thirties. The site was purchased and had planning permission granted for a redevelopment that provides 462 new home by the developer.

The consented scheme included a row of 12 detached houses, consisting of a mix of four and five bedroom units. The design of these units was rejected by the developer.

I was appointed to redesign the dwellings to a client approved brief and undertake the project through concept and design development stages.

Consented Design

Street Visualisation

The developed rejected the consented designs due to concerns with structural feasibility and saleability. This was mainly due to numerous large cantilevers on the sloped site and the sporadic arrangement of rooms. Concerns also existed regarding the extent of timber cladding on the upper level, primarily in the longevity of the material as it is presented on all four sides, creating future maintenance issues.
