UI Design

Bauhaus Website

Envisioned as a theoretical design project during the operative years of the Bauhaus school in Dessau, the business card set captures the Bauhaus spirit of innovation by redefining their purpose.

The design gains additional functionality as an identification card within the school, with the hierarchical structure identified as a set of primary colours.

The Bauhaus branding has been revisited, capturing elements of the Joschmi typographical form. The logo unifies 'bau' and 'haus' as one by blurring the boundaries between “b” and “h” in a simplistic fashion.

User Interface

Design

The design features a simplistic monochrome palette with a UI that wraps around the content. Colour is used sparingly to highlight when the user mouseovers a product, this is done in part to give the user focus and to respect each individual artwork piece.

The bauhaus|collectiv logo combines two weights of the Recross Quadratic font to create a modern and sleek typographical brand. The subheading features a fading Xants Regular font specifically chosen to emphasise the line “Own a piece of history”. The side patterning is inspired by the typological form of the Joschmi font.

This project was completed as an entry into the Adobe Hidden Treasures design competition.
